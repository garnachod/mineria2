The Week’s Trailers: Bond Faces His Dark Secret in Spectre

We got some really exciting stuff in the trailer bin this week. Bond 24, alias Spectre, finally showed real signs of life with its first teaser. The geniuses behind the marketing campaign for Mad Max continued to make our salivary glands lose control in anticipation of May, and Patrick Stewart gave us our first dose of Blunt Talk, his new show on Starz. Yes! Weekly installments of Patrick Stewart! HUZZAH! Other highlights include Joel Edgerton going full bad guy and Dolph Lundgren going full Neeson in search of his daughter, who’s been spirited away to Thailand and sold into sex trafficking. Bad news for Dolph’s character, but great news for anyone who loves rogues-of-a-certain-age setting out on global missions of vengeance and ass-kicking. That’s not the full story, of course, but we don’t love spoilers here in the trailer roundup, so watch on to uncover the details for yourself.

The One Everyone Is Talking About: Spectre (Above)

For some reason, the best word we can think of for this trailer is delicious. We’ve got immediate references to the events of Skyfall. We’ve got Monica Bellucci. We’ve got brilliantly tailored suits and handsomely fitted ribbed sweaters. And we’ve got the promise of the biggest pre-title sequence “in Bond history.” Like we said: delicious!
Pause at: 0:08 for what seems to be the shell of MI6. Stop at 0:27 for mysterious memories! Then pause at 0:32, 0:46, 0:50, and 0:59 for all kinds of spy-world tastiness.
Song: Ian Rich Orchestra, “James Bond Theme”
Essential Quote: “You’ve got a secret, something you can’t tell anyone, because you don’t trust anyone.”—Eve Moneypenny (Naomie Harris)

The One We Can’t Stop Talking About: Mad Max: Fury Road

Mad Max gets its third trailer, and we get more plot and character details! We’re. Getting. So. CLOSE!
Pause at: All the seconds.
Essential Quote: “It is by my hand you arise from the ashes of this world!”—Immortan Joe (Hugh Keays-Byrne)

The Small Screen Standout: Blunt Talk

Starz has apparently made the show of our dreams. Patrick Stewart plays Walter Blunt, a British news anchor with a at least a casual coke habit and a tendency to drink on the job who gets caught with prostitutes in cars and paints nudes of his house man. Blunt seems kind of like a freakshow cross between Will McAvoy and Archer, and he’s definitely not Professor X! The worst part about this show is that it’s only two seasons and 20 episodes, because we already want to watch it for the rest of time.
Pause at: 0:07, 0:11, 0:14, and 0:17 for the various interests of Walter Blunt. And stop at 0:27 for the … annoying hot sauce guy from the AT&T commercials?
Song: The Blue Van, “I’m a Man”
Essential Quote: “I’m Walter Blunt. Right here. Right now.”—Walter Blunt (Stewart, who we will take anywhere, any time.)

The True Story: The 33

So normally when you’re watching a Chilean movie about a Chilean story you don’t expect a script entirely in English, but guess what English speakers? This one is for you! The 33 is based on the true events that transpired when 33 men got trapped underground in a collapsed mine in Chile and then miraculously survived. Despite the fact that we know how it ends, The 33 looks to be sufficiently harrowing, with intense turns by Antonio Banderas, Lou Diamond Phillips, Juliette Binoche, Gabriel Byrne, and Rodrigo Santoro.
Pause at: 0:04, 0:35, 1:10, 1:18, 2:19, and 2:22 for incredibly emotional moments.
Essential Quote: “No! I believe we will make it out of here because I choose to believe it! All 33 of us!”—Mario Sepúlveda (Banderas).

The Sundance One: The D Train

D Train was one of a handful of well-received comedies at Sundance this year that featured very noteworthy sex scenes (see also: The Bronze, The Overnight). Jack Black plays good guy (and high school loser) Dan Landsman. Landsman is trying to rally support among his graduating class for the big reunion coming up and finds what he believes to be the perfect person to build momentum for the event if he can just get him to say yes. See, Landsman went to school with Oliver Lawless (James Marsden), who just landed in a national commercial for Banana Boat, aka the big time. Will Lawless give in and hit up the high school reunion with Landsman, thereby making him the ultimate cool guy and setting off a night of redemptive raging? Or will he usurp his marriage, home, and career? Guess you better watch and find out! Kathryn Hahn and Jeffrey Tambor co-star, and that is always great news.
Pause at: 0:31 for true stardom. Stop at 1:22, 1:55, and 2:14 for Dan living the dream…?
Song: Orchestral Manoeuvres in the Dark, “So In Love” and Welshly Arms, “That Voodoo”
Essential Quote: “He’s a big Hollywood actor now. We get Lawless to come, the whole class will follow him. It’s like one of those charity events. They bring out the celebrities. Dave Schwimmer goes, everyone goes!”—Dan Landsman (Black)

The Documentary: Amy

Oh my God this is going to be tragic!
Pause at: 0:06, 0:13, 0:16, and 0:40 to see what existed before Amy Winehouse’s youth and vitality were sucked away. We’re definitely not crying right now.
Song: Amy Winehouse, “Back to Black”
Essential Quote: “I don’t think I’m gonna be at all famous. I don’t think I could handle that! I would probably go mad! Do you know what I mean? I would go mad.”—Amy Winehouse

The Taken One: Skin Trade

So, it’s Taken, but with Dolph Lundgren? Sure! They even sprinkled on some Ron Perlman and Peter Weller to sweeten the deal.
Pause at:  0:23. Still built like a tank. Behold the man at 0:36. Sweet moves at 0:40. That scowl at 1:05! More sweet moves at 1:14.
Essential Quote: “Whatever it takes, I’m going to find my daughter.”—Detective Nick Cassidy (Lundgren, who’s still so handsome after all these years)

The Creepy One: The Gift

So Joel Edgerton doesn’t normally invoke thoughts of Scary Stalker Guy, but it looks like he’s really turning on the sadistic charm for The Gift. Edgerton plays Gordo, a man deeply affected by mysterious events of the past that involve Simon (Jason Bateman), who was a high school classmate of his. Simon and Robyn (Rebecca Hall) are newly married and looking pretty well-heeled, but when Gordo shows up everything starts coming apart in very troubling ways. Gordo has even managed to freak out real live film journalists by sending them eerie, very personal presents in the mail, signed with the name @YourFriendGordo. Can’t say we’re sorry we missed out on this scary-ass promo campaign, but it definitely spikes our interest in what The Gift has to offer.
Pause at: 0:24 for an unfriendly reaction. Looks like Simon has made a nice life for himself at 0:29. It looks like Gordo didn’t come here to make friends at 0:51, 1:08, 1:43, and 2:18.
Essential Quote: “You think you’re done with the past, but the past is not done with you.”—Gordo (Edgerton)

The Biopic: Bessie

Are y’all ready to see Queen Latifah deliver a soul-crushing performance as legendary blues singer Bessie Smith, because we are! This movie is going to be sexy and sticky and super emotional, because it’s about the blues, and because the Queen always sets it off.
Pause at: 0:01 for Queen poise. Stop at 0:04, 0:30, 0:52, 0:59 for that hard life.
Song: Bessie Smith, “Preach The Blues” and Lupe Fiasco and Guy Sebastian, “Battle Scars”
Essential Quote: “The blues is not about people knowing you. It’s about you knowing people.”—Ma Rainey (Mo’Nique)
